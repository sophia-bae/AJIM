FAQs for Job Applicants

If your questions are not answered, please contact us.

General

Who can access my data and what exactly can they view?

The goal of the system is to facilitate the online application and hiring process; only people involved in the process can have access to your data and you can control how much they can access. Your reference writers only have access to your general coversheet info and the list of jobs you have applied to. The employers have access to all the data/files listed on your Application Status page. Other employers where you haven't applied for any jobs don't have access to any of your info, unless you are on the public "Job Wanted" list.

What does it mean for a document to be "in use"?

The phrase, "in use", means that the file was submitted to a job application. You will not be able to delete it, but you can always update it. The "in use" status is cleared once the employer finishes with the job.

I have trouble uploading my official transcript.

It is most likely that the PDF file for your official transcript is encrypted or signed, and we are not allowed to use it to generate your application package. One workaround is to scan and upload an unofficial version of the file.

How do I upload a video file to my application portfolio?

If the video file is smaller than 10MB in size and it is in the standard HTML5 supported media formats or other commonly used formats, you can upload it directly just like any other files. The best way is to upload larger video files to some other video hosting sites such as YouTube. Next, you can go to your Portfolio to add a new entry, where you can click on the "type in text/url/html directly here" link in the web form to enter the URL of your uploaded video in the text box provided. Please make sure the video is available while your job applications are being reviewed.

How do I upload large or audio/video files?

You can upload as many files as needed to your Portfolio, but each file has to be less than 10MB in size, and they can be audio/video files in the standard HTML5 supported media formats or other commonly used formats. To upload a file larger than 10MB, you can either split it into smaller parts and upload each part (if the application form allows multiple files), or upload the file to some other website and provide an URL to it in the file upload form. For scanned documents, you can also try to scan it in a lower resolution or use a small file size format. You can also try to select "PDF Reduce" from the "Help" menu to help you reduce the file size first.

How can I be notified of any new position postings?

There are 3 ways you can access the latest postings:
  1. Go to the job listing page. Click on the 'select a discipline' link near the top to find the sections you are interested in. Click on the links there to subscribe to the RSS feeds, and your RSS reader will let you know any new postings.
  2. Access our twitter feed to see the new postings.
  3. Create a free job applicant account and login as an applicant, then select 'Search' link near the top to search for the positions you are interested in. Save the searches and check the box for "email notify of new jobs" on daily, weekly or monthly basis. The system will then email you as requested.

How do I combine two accounts?

You just need to login to the account you don't want to keep, then select 'Change Email' from the 'Help' menu. Once they've been combined, all the reference letters, portfolio files and applications in both accounts will appear in the existing account.

How do I delete a file?

To replace/update/delete a file, just click on the entry name in your Portfolio listing to bring up the edit form. You can't delete a file in your Portfolio if it's in use. To just remove the file from your application, go to your Status page and click on the full job title of the application to bring up the application form. On the form, unselect (by selecting 'Select One') the file from the dropdowns near the bottom of the form, and submit.

Why is it necessary to convert every file into PDF?

1. PDF files can be viewed easily on the most systems, and they can be easily rearranged.
2. We need to make sure any uploaded PDF files are free of errors so they can be correctly viewed everywhere; some PDF files do contain serious errors even though they may be perfectly viewable on some systems.
3. For identification, we need to embed a simple watermark in the PDF files.

I hit the hide button for my cover letter by mistake. How do I get it to reappear? ("Un-hide Option")

At the end of the help message near the top of the page, there is a 'show all' link, click on it to show all items, then click on the green arrow to unhide it.

Why do I see two links like "Word, PDF" for each uploaded file?

For each uploaded file, we list 2 links for you to access the same file (maybe in different formats). The main reason is that we convert every file in the system to a PDF format in order to make sure they are readable by most readers and fix some common problems in the original files. The 1st link is always the original file, and the 2nd link the converted PDF file. You can access the file using either one.

I uploaded a file but the PDF file generated by the system is not correct. What should I do?

The system uses an automated process to convert your file to a PDF format and the process sometimes may not output acceptable result due to fonts or other issues. If this happens, you can try to save your file in PDF format on your own system and then upload the PDF file directly, or follow the on-screen instructions (the last sentence of the red text block after submitting the form) to minimize the conversion and see if it works. To try the latter on a file you have already uploaded, go to your Portfolio page to click on the entry name for that file to bring up the uploading form, simply press 'Upload' button, and then click on the link inside the red text block.

I was told I have two account with the same name and same email address, how to cancel one of them?

The proper thing to do is to use the one with the right email address exclusively from now on and forget about the other one. You don't have to delete or cancel it.

I didn't receive my login token.

This could be due to that your email system is slow or the message was caught as spam by your spam filter. You can search your spam folder for messages from support@academicjobsonline.org to see if there.

How do I change my password?

One way is to use the 'Forgot your password?' link on the login page if you want the system to set a random one for you. Another way is to select 'New Password' from the 'Help' menu if you have already logged in.

Can I hide my name and contact info on the "Job Wanted" list?

Yes, just check the 'hide my name' and 'hide my contact info' in your standard coversheet. The employer can always fill out a form to email you in case they are interested.

How to clear the web browser's cache?

This happens sometimes. You need to clear your web browser's cache to force it to 'forget' the old file. If you see the message "POST Form ID is not valid for this submission, please reload....", clearing the cache should also fix it.

How to reset my forgotten password?

To get a new password, go to the applicant login page, click on the 'Forgot your password?' link and enter your email address. A new password will be emailed to you right away. If you are a new user, and forget your password before entering the login token, you can start over fresh by going to the login page and enter your email, a new password and check the "this is my first login, please create an account for me." checkbox.

Can I add my own annotation to a job?

Yes, click on the wand-like icon next to the job to do it. You can mark it as interested, add a private note or add your own deadline.

What should I enter for the Last Name on the Coversheet if I don't have a last name?

Please enter a " . " or " - " for the last name. The system will try to display it properly on your applications.

Applications

"Incomplete (1 of 3 Required Reference Letters)"

This message indicates that application is incomplete, with some required items missing. In this example, the job requires at least 3 reference letters, you have only 2 in the system and you need to have 1 more. Please note if some of your application material were sent by postal mail, they may not be counted for in the system even though the school may have all the items.

Submitting Final Application

Once you have submitted your application (once) with all required material attached/selected and referees identified, there is no need to submit it again to make it 'final'. Any newly uploaded reference letters by those identified referees will be automatically added to the application; so are any updates in your Portfolio to the attached/selected material. You can always verify your applications by going to the 'Status' page and clicking on the employers' names to see (mostly) what the employers see. You need to submit the application again only if you have updates to the questions in the application, new files to add or change the list of referees.

Can I delete the files in my Portfolio once I've submitted the application?

No, do not delete the files for the existing applications. To apply for new positions, you need to upload new files or reuse the existing files. You can choose different references on the application form too. But please remember that the search committees access your application material directly from your Portfolio for those files you have submitted in the job applications, hence you can't delete them.

How to update my existing job application?

If you only need to update the submitted files to the application, go to your Portfolio page, click on the 1st link on the line of the file you want to update to upload the new file in its place. If you also need to change your answers to some of the questions on the application form, go to the Status page and click on the full job title to bring up the application form; make your changes and submit. Remember to always verify your application by going to your Status page.

What to do if submitting the application form results in "Internal Server Error"?

The most likely cause might be either the files you have selected to be uploaded on the application form are too large or some of the files are not acceptable to the system. Another cause might be that your web browser (such as some old versions of IE and Safari) doesn't upload files properly.

The workaround is to upload the files you need one by one before submitting the application itself: after login click on the 'Portfolio' menu at the top to go to your Portfolio page (that's where you keep all your files). Click on category name links such as 'Cover Letter' there or 'add your own...' link to upload your files. If any of your PDF files are larger than 10MB, you can try to select "PDF Reduce" from the "Help" menu to see if you can reduce the file sizes.

Once you have all your files uploaded into your Portfolio, please don't forget to actually complete the application form for the job you want to apply in the first place, by finding the job on the Jobs list and clicking on the 'Apply' or 'Applied' link there to go back to the application form. On the application form you can just select those already uploaded files directly from your Portfolio using the dropdown menus there, and submit the application form by pressing the "Apply" or "Update" button.

When is the deadline for a position?

The deadline is 23:59PM in the US Eastern Time on the deadline day. You can move your mouse over the deadline on a job ad page to see this. It's also displayed in red in your local time on the deadline day (if the deadline is enforced) or the display date.

Will employers see my updated file?

The employer will always see the latest version of the files you submitted in the job application. No need to reapply. You can alway verify this by clicking on an employer's name on your 'Status' page, which shows you what each employer actually see (with the only differences that they can view the contents of your reference letters, and they can't see the file names of your letters and your portfolio items submitted.)

Update Application Information

You are updating the existing information. The original application date stays the same. Since the employers access the files submitted in a job application *directly* from your 'Portfolio', they are always acccessing the latest you have. There is no need to re-submit if you have only updated some files used in your applications. You need to re-submit if the answers to any questions in the application form need to be changed; for example, you want to select a different set of reference writers, choose different research interests or select a different file from your Portfolio for your CV.

Same thing is true for reference letters: no need to re-submit just because the selected reference writers have just uploaded more letters. The reference writers have control of which employers can access the letters, and the right employers automatically get the new letters.

As always, to verify that the correct info was received by the right employer, click on the employer's name in your 'Status' page.

Multiple Cover Letters

You should have different names for different items. Suppose you submited "Cover_Letter_Duke" to Duke, and later one you click on the same entry "Cover_Letter_Duke" to uploaded a revised version to replace it, the one Duke sees will be the revised one since they are the same entry and the old one has been replaced and it's gone. But if you keep the old entry alone, and upload a new one with different name, the old one you have submited to schools will remain to be the one seen by schools.

If I applied online to a job, do I need to let the employer know that I applied or AJO does it?

No need to let the employer know. Employers should know that from this site.

How to upload more files than the job ad asked?

Unfortunately there is no way to submit more items then those asked in the job ads. One way to get around this is to put the reprints etc on the web somewhere and put a link in your cover letter to that page. If you want to submit multiple PDF files for one required item, you can try to select "PDF Reduce" from the "Help" menu to concatenate the files into one PDF file first and submit the combined file.

Reference letters are different, as there is no limit on the number of letters you can submit to a job application.

How to save application in progress without submitting it?

The application in progress should be automatically saved in your web browser; and when you return to the same application form, it'll ask you if you want to use the saved data.

You can also save it directly to the server by pressing the "Save" button on the application form. Please note that saved applications are not submitted and the employers can't access them at all; please don't forget to actually submit them by pressing "Apply" or "Update" button when you are ready.

I don't have a Publication List

The best way to avoid an incomplete message is to create a portfolio entry with text "The publication list is included in my CV" and submit that to the "publication list" request.

How can I reopen my cover sheet in order to correct errors?

To reopen your cover sheet, click on the 'Portfolio' link at the top, then click on the "Standard CoverSheet" to bring up the form where you can make changes.

How can I delete an application completely?

You can delete an application completely by going to the application screen and press 'Withdraw', within 2 days of the initial application. After 2 days, you can only mark the application as 'withdrawn' and you can't remove it from your 'Status' page.

The job I want to apply was listed before I logged in. It's gone once I logged in.

Each job is classified under some general disciplines such as 'English' or 'Physics'. The job won't be displayed if its discipline doesn't match what you have selected in your Standard Coversheet. Please go to this link to select more disciplines if this happens.

How can I tell which jobs I have applied to so far?

All current jobs you have applied are listed in your 'Status' page. If it's not there, either you haven't applied or the job is finished.

What's the interview signaling for?

The "interview signaling" is an additional way for applicants to signal their unusual interest in a particular position they are applying in order to improve their chances to get an interview. Currently not all employers use this feature, and you won't find it on all the application forms.

Sending an interview signal is a way for you to let the group know that you are particularly interested in this position. Signal could be used for a position you are interested in but which would likely not interview you without it, since they might otherwise doubt whether you are likely to take their offer if one is made after interview. The search committee can also use the signals to break ties among similar applicants. You could use a signal to communicate your interest if you don't have any other better ways to do it for this position. You can only send 2 signals among all the applications you make on this system; you can't unselect the signal after 2 days once this has been submitted, unless you withdraw your application or until 6 months later or after the position has been finished.

This mechanism allows applicants to designate up to 2 of their applications in the system as 'particularly interested in' ('send 2 signals'). Applicants can only have 2 signals at any time and they can't be changed within 6 months after being set. Some applicants may choose not to send any. Signaling is only useful if this position usually receives many applications and applicants in your disciplines tend to apply for many other positions, and your search committee wants 'one more hint' beyond the usual application materials to see if the applicant is more than likely to accept the offer if one is made after interview. This is one more way for the applicant to 'grab your attention' among similarly qualified applicants, and it could lead your search committee to consider those applicants who appear to be over-qualified (who traditionally won't accept your offer) or slightly less ideal. Please keep in mind that not sending a signal conveys almost no information and it shouldn't be used by your search committee in their decision against an applicant.

References

How does the reference submission process work?

First you enter all the references you need for all your applications on your standard coversheet form, and the system will create reference writer accounts for them if they are new. You can check the box "email notify on submit" or "email again" next to a reference writer and submit the form, or click on the green arrow (if there), if you want the system send a letter request email to the writer. Once the writers have received the email requests, they can use the quick upload link or the password in the email to login and upload their letters easily. Please note the system doesn't send any letter request email until you request it.

Please note that each reference writer you entered in your coversheet must have a unique (i.e. different) and valid email address. They can't share the same email address. Writers are identified by their email addresses only in the system; please don't change their email addresses by yourself if they have already uploaded any letters. You can remove a writer from your reference list just by simply clearing the entry from your coversheet if you don't have any active applications where the writer is used and which don't allow any updates after deadlines. A staff person (or a school dossier service) can upload letters for multiple faculty members if you select the proper checkbox in the coversheet; please see the FAQ https://academicjobsonline.org/ajo/faqs/0/21.

Reference writers normally only need to submit the letters once. The best time to let the database email your referees is after you have applied a few jobs. Please allow the writers enough time to write the letter before the application deadlines.

To check if a referee has uploaded any letters for you, go to your coversheet page. All uploaded letters will be listed there in red text. Or you can go to your Status page to see which letters each employer can access.

How to notify my reference writers to request for letters?

All you have to do is go to your Coversheet form, then check the 'notify by email on submit' or 'email again' checkbox for that referee and submit the form, or simply click on the green arrows there for existing references. Your reference will then receive an email with login instructions. They can't login until you send them the email. Please note the system won't send any reference letter requests until requested.

Another way for the referees to request the same email is for the referee (or you) to go to the reference login page and click on the "Request a new email message with upload info?" link.

Generally you only need to do this once per job season; no need to notify them for every job you are applying

How to know if the reference letter request email has actually been sent?

To check if the reference letter request email messages you have requested have actually been delivered by our mail server to their destinations, go to your coversheet form and click on the green arrows there. Please note even though the email messages should be sent right away, but the delivery status info will be available to you with a possible 2 hour delay.

How to make my reference letters uploaded in Interfolio to be accessible to the schools I'm applying here?

A similar FAQ is also available at the Interfolio site. You are strongly advised not to request Interfolio to mail/email any letters directly to the employers or to us, and we respectfully ignore all such requests.

Please note: You will need to upgrade your Interfolio account to the Dossier Deliver subscription for access to sending materials.

We have an office staff who handles all reference letters. Is there a way to have the office staff upload my letters?

There are 2 ways to do this:

#1. You enter all your faculty letter writers, each with a different and unique email address, in your standard coversheet page. [If a staff is only responsible for uploading one reference writer's letters, you can just use that staff's email address, instead of the writer's actual email, and the writer's name in the reference entry, and skip this FAQ.] The letter writers can designate the same office staff to be their proxy and then the office staff will receive all letter requests and can upload letters on their behalf.

To do that, the writers have to login to enter the office staff's email as their proxy. It's really easy for them to do; just login and click on the 'proxy' link near the bottom. They only need to do this once and the proxy works for all applicants they may have. Or the staff person can request to be added as a proxy for a faculty member by clicking on the 'Add as a proxy?' link on the reference login page.

If the faculty member doesn't want to add the staff person as his/her proxy, he can simply forward the reference request emails to the staff person, and the staff person can click on the links in the emails to upload the requested letters.

#2. If you really don't want to bother your writers directly (or you are dealing with a letter service), you need to enter the office staff's name and email, as a new reference entry, in your standard coversheet page only once, AND check the "must check here if the person above will upload letters on behalf of multiple writers:" checkbox. To email the login info to the staff person, check the "email notify writer on submit?" checkbox and submit. Using this option, there is no need to enter each actual faculty reference writers on your coversheet at all; only one entry for the staff person is sufficient. It's really important that you check the checkbox.

Then this staff person can login and post all your letters (by clicking on your name on the Refs or Proxies list). It's good idea to tell the person to enter the writer's full name in the upload form. If multiple letters have been scanned into a single file, the staff person MUST enter all the writers' names and separate them by semicolons(;). The last point is important so your letters will be counted properly. Please note the staff person's name and email will be listed on your job applications, but the real writer's name will be displayed after each letter uploaded. *** Note to the staff person: if the letters you are asked to upload are generic in their contents, please upload them as generic letters (i.e., not select any employers on the uploading form). That way you only need to upload the same letters once. It's also a good idea to enter an appropriate 'Entry Name' for the letter and have the "allow applicant to see the Entry Name" box checked, to make it easy for the applicants to select the right letters on their application forms. ***

Note to method #2: if you (the applicant) forgot to check the "must check here if the person above will upload letters on behalf of multiple writers" box on your coversheet form and the staff has already uploaded the letters, you'll find that system doesn't count your letters correctly on your Status page, since there is no way for the system to tell whom the actual writer is for each of the letters. To fix it, please check that box on your coversheet form for that staff and submit, *then* ask the staff person to login and click on the 1st link for each of the letters to enter the actual writer's name, check the box "keep the original uploading date unchanged", and submit.

How can I tell which reference writers have submitted their letters?

All existing letters are listed on your standard coversheet in each reference writer's entry under 'Existing Letters'. You can also see what letters each employer have by clicking on the employer's name in your Status page.

Please note in your coversheet, you can see when the letters are uploaded, when they expire and if there are any access restrictions (e.g., a letter is only for some employers, etc. This helps you to explain why different employers may have different set of letters).

If nothing is listed under a referee's entry in your coversheet form, then that referee hasn't uploaded any letters yet, or the letters were uploaded using a different email address. Please contact the referee directly to see why.

How to make old reference letters from last year not shown up on my current applications?

Go to your standard coversheet form, click on the green checkmarks after the letters you don't want to use in any applications to turn them into red X's and submit the coversheet form. Any letters marked with a red X won't be used in any of your applications.

My reference writers can't see the schools I'm going to apply to when they upload letters.

The list of schools the reference writers see when uploading letters consists of the schools you have already applied and the schools with jobs in the same disciplines you have selected in your coversheet form or schools whose jobs you have marked on the Jobs list. To add the missing school, first please find what discipline(s) the job is posted under (by looking at the job ad page), then go to your coversheet form https://academicjobsonline.org/ajo?coversheet#dis and select it to be added to your disciplines; or you can simply click on the wand-like icon on the job listing page to mark the job you are interested in.

Another way is to tell your reference writer to click on the 'all employers' link just above the list of schools on the uploading form to include all schools in the systems on the list.

How to use a letter of a different type?

This is really easy to do: go to the job listing/description page for the position you are applying (for example this link) and click on the small yellow wand-like icon there to bring up a dialog form. Set your own position type to "Tenure-track faculty" (for example) and press "Save". Then the letter will be available on the application form for you to use.

Are there deadlines for reference letter submissions?

AJO doesn't set any deadlines for reference letter submitting; they can be submitted even after the deadlines of the positions you are applying. But the employers might not consider your applications if they don't receive all the required material by the deadline. You can contact the employers directly to make sure late reference letters are acceptable.

How to import existing reference letters from mathjobs.org?

First of all, create your accounts at MathJobs.Org and academicjobsonline.org using exactly the same email address, and enter the reference writers on your coversheets at the both places using exactly the same email addresses too. Once that's done, go back to your coversheet form, then you can click on the "import generic letters from MathJobs.Org" link for each reference writer to import the letters. This also works the other way around to get generic letters from academicjobsonline.org to MathJobs.Org.

Can I use different letters for different positions within the same employer department?

Yes, the most important aspect is to make sure the reference writers have uploaded different letters for the different positions. On the application form to each of the positions click on the green arrows for those reference writers you want to request letters for this position and send them a tailored request. Please note the application form won't have any green arrows if the position doesn't require any actual reference letters. Once the writers have received the request, they can click on a link in the email to upload their tailored letter for this position. The writers have to make sure the right employer and position are selected on the letter uploading form in order for the system to use the letter properly. You can verify the letters have been uploaded for a given position only by going to your coversheet form or the Status page.

Above all, you can also use different sets of reference writers for different positions in the same department.

Can I send my reference letters to employers not using AJO?

Yes, we have an eDelivery service for that. For it to work, first please ask the employer to sign up for the free eDelivery service. Once we have created an account for the employer, you can login to create an eDelivery package to them.

Another option is to send the letters to the Interfolio services if you are familiar with Interfolio. You can send the letters as an eDelivery package to them.

A job I'm applying to doesn't require any acutal reference letters, can I still submit them to it?

No, the employer won't be able to see any of your reference letters. You can verify this from your Status page. If the letters are optional or acceptable but not required, you can submit your letters to it if you like.

Even though the letters are not required or used for this job, but if you want your reference writers to upload their letters now in case they'll be required later, you can send them generic letter requests from your coversheet form https://academicjobsonline.org/ajo/coversheet#ref, and tell them to select the employer on the letter uploading form to upload a tailored letter for it.

Should I wait for the reference letters to be uploaded before applying for jobs?

You don't have to have all the references ready before applying. The letters will be automatically available to the employers whenever they are uploaded, even after the deadline. Also, any newly added reference writers in your standard coversheet will be automatically added to all the jobs you have already applied, unless otherwise configured in your coversheet.

I'm applying for two jobs throught your system. Do my referees write one generic reference letter that is used by both schools, or do they upload two personalized letters?

They can do either way. For each letter, they can set the schools it's for, or not set access permission to make it generic.

To upload a personalized letter for a given school (or a list of schools), the reference writer clicks on the green arrow in the "Access permissions" section of the upload form, then selects the schools from the list of schools.

Can my letter writers see my portfolio?

Your letter writers can't see your whole portfolio. They can only see your stanard coversheet and the list of places you are applying.

What email address should I arrange for the references to be sent to?

The reference letters are expected to be submitted online at this site, unless instructed otherwise in the job ad. To do that, enter all your references' names and email addresses on your standard coversheet under Portfolio, make sure checking the 'email notify on submit?' checkbox and submit the form. Your references will receive an email with login info and a link to submit the letters. Once this is done, you should be able to select the references you want for each employer in your job applications.

How to remove a reference writer?

To remove a reference writer from your portfolio, go to the Standard CoverSheet form under your Portfolio, remove the reference's email and name there, and submit the form.

To remove a reference letter from your application, go to the Status page, then click on the full job title of the application to bring up the application form. On the application form, uncheck the box in front of the reference writer and submit the form.

References Disappeared

More than likely, you have unselected all references when you applied for the position. Please go to your "Status" page, click on the job title to bring up the application form, select your referees, and resubmit the form. Another thing is that you should never change the referee's email address in your coversheet once they have uploaded any letters.

How to send my reference writer a new password?

Go to this link and fill in his email address you entered in your coversheet and submit. He'll receive an email right away.

If he also lost the email with login and upload instructions, you can go to your standard coversheet page under your Portfolio and check the 'Notify again?' checkbox next to his name and submit. A link is included in the email which he can just click to submit your letter, without knowing any passwords.

Is there any advantage to ask my referees to send their letters by postal mail or email?

Almost certainly not. Most time the employers have to scan and upload the letters into your applications, or the search committee members might miss your letters if they are stored offline in a file cabinet.

'Word/Wrtr' & 'PDF/Schl' for My References

'Wrtr' there means the letter was uploaded by the reference writer directly; 'Schl' means the letter was scanned and uploaded by the school you are applying.

How to select letters for positions with different types or without type set?

This is really easy to do: As an example let's say your referee uploaded a letter and tagged it as for postdoctoral positions only, but the job you are applying has no position type set or has a different type but you really want to use this postdoctoral letter for that position. You can arrange it by going to the position ad page (for example this link) and set the position's type to postdoctoral. You do that by clicking on the wand-like icon there.

When do I check the "concerns teaching" checkbox next to a reference writer on the coversheet?

In AJO, most reference writers are expected to write about the applicant's research in their letters. If you have selected someone to talk mainly about your teaching abilities, you can have the "concerns teaching" checkbox checked. It's a clear indication to the reviewers that this is a teaching letter.

Focus : Reference Letters

Can I upload a copy of a reference letter for my reference writer?

No. Your reference writer is the only person who can upload a reference letter.

Can I extend the expiration dates on my reference letters?

No. Your reference writer is the only person who can make changes to the expiration date of a reference letter.

Did my reference letters get submitted to AcademicJobsOnline?

To find out whether your reference letters have been submitted to AcademicJobsOnline, click on the the Status link in the header or footer of the AJO website after you've logged in. Then click on the name of the department, which will bring up your application to that department. You will be able to see which reference letters have been received for your application.

The references you submitted are listed in the References section, about 2/3 of the way down the page. To the right of each reference is Received, where a date will be listed only if the reference letter has been received. This Date Received item will be your indication as to whether the department has received a reference letter from this listed reference.

What happens if my reference writer refuses to use AcademicJobsOnline?

In that case, give your reference writer information about sending in the letter via regular mail. You can also indicate this on your cover sheet in the References section, where there is a checkbox advising that a reference writer has requested to send the letter by postal mail.

Will AcademicJobsOnline notify my reference writers that they need to submit their reference letters?

Assuming your reference writer does not limit access to the letter, it should be viewable to any AcademicJobsOnline employer to whom you made an application. On your cover sheet in the References section, you can check the box, email or email again, which will send instructions to your reference writer.

How can I submit more reference letters than the school requires?

First, enter all references on your cover sheet, then on the Job Application page choose as many references as you wish for that particular job.

Can you tell me how to specify who my reference writers are on the AcademicJobsOnline site without automated emails being sent to them requesting references?

List your references on your cover sheet and do not check the box that says, email notify on submit.

Focus : Applicant Portfolio

I have created a new portfolio by using a new email address, how do I delete my old portfolio?

If you wish to continue using the new Portfolio, the old portfolio will be automatically deleted 12 (twelve) months after the last log in.

If you wish to use the old portfolio and you have not applied for any jobs using the new portfolio, you can log into the new account and use the Caution... Delete my account right now option on the cover sheet. After completing this step, log into the old account (using old email login). Then go to the cover sheet page, change your email address and click on the Submit button.

How do I go about deleting my portfolio from AcademicJobsOnline completely?

Your portfolio will automatically be deleted 12 months after your last login.

Alternatively, on your cover sheet, where it says, Remove my account and all files once all the jobs I've applied for are finished, you may check this box if you wish to remove all your data from the system (essentially "deleting yourself").

This will cause your files to be deleted 3 months after all the jobs you've applied for are marked "finished" by the employer. For applicants who never apply for any jobs, checking this box will cause an account to be deleted after 6 months.

How long does my portfolio stay on AcademicJobsOnline?

Old applicant accounts will automatically be deleted after 6 months after all jobs applied for are finished.

An account is deleted after 3 months if it is marked to be removed once the applicant's jobs are finished (see previous question above) — or if it is marked as "off market" by the applicant.

If an applicant hasn't tried to login for 12 months, the account will be deleted at that point.

I am having trouble uploading documents to my portfolio. Are there special formats?

Yes, here is a list of the acceptable formats: PDF, Word, Postscript, DVI, GIF, JPEG, LaTex, Tex, RTF, URL, HTML, TEXT (use the URL format with caution). If you still continue to have problems, then contact us for help.

Which files from my portfolio do employers have access to when I apply for a particular job?

Employers only have access to the files that you have selected when applying for that particular job.

Focus : Password Issues

I am setting up a new account, but AcademicJobsOnline keeps rejecting my chosen password?

Currently a password has to be at least 6 characters long and cannot contain a simple dictionary word or a simple sequence of characters next to each other on the keyboard. For example, 'application' and 'qwerty' are not valid passwords, whereas 'applic1tion' and 'qwexrty' are valid.

What is the procedure to change the password that was given to me?

Go to the Login page, enter your email address and your current password, then where it says, new password... if you want to change, enter a new password of your choice. — Please remember that a password has to be at least 6 characters long and cannot contain a simple dictionary word or a simple sequence of characters next to each other on the keyboard. For example, 'application' and 'qwerty' are not valid passwords, whereas 'applic1tion' and 'qwexrty' are valid.

I cannot remember my password. How do I get a new one?

Go to the Login page and click the link to "Forgot your password?"". This will take you to a new screen where you can provide your email address. After you click Submit, AcademicJobsOnline will send you an email with a new password.

If you check the box next to List me in the "Job Wanted" (or "Free Agent") list (located near the bottom) when you create your cover sheet, your cover sheet can only be seen by participating AcademicJobsOnline employers. If you do not check that box, your cover sheet can be seen only by employers to whom you have made an application.

The new information will be viewable to the employer — however, you have no way of knowing when an employer might visit the screen. Therefore you may wish to notify the employer that you have updated the file.

Focus : Applying for Jobs

Can an application be submitted by email toAcademicJobsOnline?

No, AcademicJobsOnline is an automated job system. You will need to register for an account. Once you have registered, you may then apply for a job using the AcademicJobsOnline system.

Can I receive emails about new job postings?

Yes. Go to the "Search" page and search for the jobs that you are interested in, or just click Submit for all the jobs. You can then save the search by giving it a name and check the email notify of new jobs checkbox to get the email when anything new fits the search. You can save as many searches as you like.

Some employers request material that is not on the list of items I am automatically asked to submit. How do I include extra items that are not on that list?

You must contact the school directly.

How can I withdraw an application either during the active application period, or after jobs have finished?

During the active application period, go to your "Status" screen and select the job title of the school for which you have applied. At the bottom of that screen, use the withdraw button.

Once a job is marked as finished by the employer, you are not able to withdraw your application. AcademicJobsOnline give employers the right to access records of completed job searches.

How can I delete the list of old job applications that I see on my status screen?

You cannot delete the list. However, the old job applications will disappear after the employer marks the jobs as finished or deleted and six months have passed.
